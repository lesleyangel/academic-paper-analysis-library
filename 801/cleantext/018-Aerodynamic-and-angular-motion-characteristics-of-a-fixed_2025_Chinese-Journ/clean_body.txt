## 1. Introduction1

Spin-stabilized projectiles have been widely deployed in recent years, but their attack precision remains limited.1 To enhance hit accuracy, the fixed-canard dual-spin projectile, designed as a type of trajectory-corrected projectile, has been developed. It utilizes the gyroscopic stability generated by the high-speed spinning of the aft body to maintain flight stability. Additionally, it can correct the trajectory by controlling the rotation of the front canard, thereby improving attack precision.2,3

Due to the high-speed rotation of the aft body, the dual-spin projectile exhibits highly nonlinear and unsteady aerodynamic characteristics, along with strong couplings between the motion of the lateral and normal axes.2,4 The projectile’s flight involves coupled angular motions—spin, precession, and nutation— resulting in highly complex dynamic behavior.5 When the projectile’s spinning rate is quite low, it may lead to insufficient gyroscopic stability, thereby compromising flight stability. Conversely, when the spinning rate is too high, it may reduce trajectory-following stability, causing the projectile to experience an excessively large angle of attack during flight, which negatively affects the stability of its angular motion.6 Thus, an in-depth investigation of the aerodynamic and angular motion characteristics of the dual-spin projectile is necessary. Moreover, exploring the underlying flow and dynamic mechanisms of the angular motion can provide valuable insights for the advancement of future control system designs.

With the continuous development of computer technology, Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) has broad applications in analysis of aeronautics and astronautics.7 Many researches7-11 have explored the aerodynamic characteristics of projectiles with canards. Li et al.12 predicted the flow field and aerodynamic coefficients of a spin-stabilized projectile with a correction fuze via numerical simulation, and they discovered that the aerodynamic coefficients and control forces generated by canards changed with the Mach number. Jaiswal et al.13 conducted numerical simulations on a 155 mm spinning projectile over a Mach number range of 0.8 to 2.5, with AoA 0° . Through the numerical simulation, they predicted the aerodynamic coefficients at different Mach numbers and analyzed the flow field structures in both the transonic and supersonic regimes. Liu et al.14 used the sliding mesh approach to study the influence of the spinning rate of the aft body, angle of attack, and Mach number, on the time-averaged aerodynamic characteristics of a dual-spin projectile through numerical simulation, revealing the underlying flow mechanisms. Yin et al.15 compared the aerodynamic coefficients of spinning projectiles using the unsteady Reynolds-Average Navier-Stokes (RANS) equation with the aerodynamic coefficients obtained from theoretical formulations, finding significant differences at high spinning rates, which indicates that the theoretical formulation method has limitations in calculating aerodynamic characteristics. In conclusion, forebody spinning significantly affects the aerodynamic characteristics of the dual-spin projectile. However, the flow mechanisms generating yawing moment and lateral force, which can lead to flight instability, remain not fully understood.

Most studies carried out on angular motion characteristics of dual-spin projectiles rely on aerodynamic interpolation tables,16,17 neglecting the unsteady aerodynamic effects. Similarly, studies on angular motion often use linearized angular motion differential equations,18,19 introducing excessive assumptions and simplifications. Moreover, when the aerodynamic characteristics extends beyond the linear regime, the small-perturbation linearity assumption is no longer valid.20 Shi and Liu21 analyzed the angular motion mechanism of the dual-spin projectile using the angle of attack equation, and the influence of the liftcanard deflection on the trajectory is also studied using aerodynamic interpolation tables and a Seven-Degreeof-Freedom (7-DOF) dynamic model. Wang et al.22 studied the angular motion of a tail rudder rocket under the influence of periodic control forces by employing a complex-plane angular motion differential equation. However, the significant coupling effect between aerodynamics and flight behaviors23 hasn’t been considered. Traditional CFD techniques cannot simulate the dynamic process of the dual-spin projectile during flight. Hence, the coupled CFD and Rigid Body Dynamics (RBD) method has been developed to investigate interactions between aerodynamics and flight dynamics.24-26

The coupled CFD/RBD method simultaneously solves the fluid flow and flight dynamics equations, capturing unsteady aerodynamic characteristics at every moment during flight with high fidelity.27,28 Yan et $\mathsf{al.} ^ {29}$ implemented the coupled CFD/RBD method to simulate the flow fields generated by missile motion, and realized accurate simulation of the pitch and elevator deflection of the missile, which demonstrates that the CFD/RBD method can accurately simulate the motion and flow field of the missile even in continuous maneuvers. Silton et al.30 compared the aerodynamic characteristics predicted by CFD and coupled CFD/RBD simulations on a canard-controlled projectile. Their results indicates that the angular resolution of coupled simulation is higher than the uncoupled simulations. However, most of the existing CFD/RBD simulations are trajectory-centered as the method does not require aerodynamic model, thus there is a lack of study analyzing aerodynamic-motion coupling effects using the CFD-RBD method.

In this paper, we performed numerical simulations of a fixed-canard dual-spin projectile under various forebody spinning rates to investigate its aerodynamic and angular motion characteristics. The unsteady CFD approach is employed to investigate the aerodynamic characteristics, as well as the canards’ interference effect of on the aerodynamic properties at different angles of attack and forebody spinning rates under fixed-axis rotation conditions. The coupled CFD/RBD method is applied to investigate the aerodynamic and angular motion characteristics during flight, as well as their coupling effects at different forebody spinning rates. The main contributions of this work are summarized as follows:

(1) The canards’ interference effect on the aerodynamic characteristics is assessed by analyzing aerodynamic characteristics and flow field structures at different forebody spinning rates and angles of attack.

(2) The influence of the forebody spinning rates on the angular motion and trajectory characteristics are analyzed by the coupled CFD/RBD method. Furthermore, the mechanism underlying the effect of the forebody spinning rate on the angular motion is explored.

## 2. Numerical method

## 2.1. CFD method

In this study, the self-developed MPI parallel flow solver HUNS3D,31,32 which can simulate both steady and unsteady flows, is employed as the primary flow solver. Since the dual-spin projectile has relative motion between the forebody and aft body, there exists interference effects between the canards and aft body. To accurately simulate the interference effect under different spinning rates, the sliding mesh technique is used in HUNS3D.

Due to the Arbitrary Lagrangian-Eulerian method (ALE) has some inherent advantages in 6-DOF simulations.33 The unsteady RANS equation formulated using the ALE method is employed as the flow governing equation in this study. The integral form of the ALEbased RANS equations is expressed as

$$\frac {\partial} {\partial t} \iiint _ {\mathcal {Q}} \mathbf {d} \mathcal {Q} + \iint _ {\hat {c} \mathcal {Q}} F \big ( \pmb {Q} , \nu _ {\mathrm{grid}} \big ) \bullet \pmb {n} \mathrm{d} S = \iint _ {\hat {c} \mathcal {Q}} \mathbf {G} ( \pmb {Q} ) \bullet \boldsymbol {n} \mathrm{d} S\tag{1}$$

where $\varOmega$ and  respectively denote the control volume and its boundary. $\pmb {Q} = \left[ \rho \rho \nu _ {x} \rho \nu _ {y} \rho \nu _ {z} \rho E \right]$ is the conserved variables, while E indicates the total internal energy of control volume, and $\rho$ represents fluid density. The flow velocities along the $x , y ,$ and z direction are represented $\mathsf{as} \quad \nu _ {x} , \quad \nu _ {y}$ and $\nu _ {z}$ respectively. The grid movement velocity, computed using a second-order backward difference scheme $, ^ {32}$ is denoted as $\pmb {\nu} _ {\mathrm{grid}}$ . n denotes the unit normal vector pointing outward from the border of the control volume. $F ( Q )$ and ${\pmb G} ( {\pmb Q} )$ respectively correspond to the inviscid fluxes and viscous fluxes. 34

In this study, an improved LU-SGS35 algorithm is adopted as the time-marching scheme, and the SA model is chosen for turbulence modeling.

## 5. Conclusions

In this study, we investigate the aerodynamic and angular motion characteristics of a fixed-canard dualspin projectile at various forebody spinning rates through numerical simulations. The unsteady aerodynamic characteristics of the dual-spin projectile spinning around fixed axis are analyzed through unsteady CFD simulations. Further, coupled CFD/RBD simulations are conducted to study the angular motion and aerodynamic characteristics with the forebody respectively spinning at fixed rates and freely. The main conclusions of this work are summarized as follows:

(1) The forebody spinning influences the lateral aerodynamic characteristics of the dual-spin projectile. An increase in the opposite-direction spinning rate of the forebody leads to a higher lateral force coefficient, while simultaneously reducing the yawing moment coefficient. As the angle of attack increases, the impact of the forebody spinning on the lateral force and yawing moment coefficients becomes more significant.

(2) When the forebody spins, the effective angle of attack differs between the left and right canards, resulting in stronger vortices on the side with larger effective angle of attack. This results in asymmetry in the wingtip vortex structures, and changes its aerodynamic characteristics.

(3) The projection of the moment perpendicular to the body axis onto the projectile axis is a key factor influencing its angular motion. When the projection is larger than zero, the amplitude of the angular motion increases gradually.

(4) When the aft body and forebody of the dual-spin projectile spin in the same direction, the amplitude of angular motion increases, leading to reduced stability. By contrast, employing a control strategy in which the forebody spins in the opposite direction or remains stationary can reduce the projection of the moment onto the projectile’s axis, thereby enhancing the stability of its angular motion.
